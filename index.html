Why This Combo Works
Turmeric + Black Pepper

Piperine in black pepper inhibits liver enzymes that would normally break down curcumin too fast — boosting its bioavailability by up to 2000%. This pairing is scientifically validated.

Ginger + Turmeric

Both block the same NF-κB inflammatory pathway but via different mechanisms — gingerols and curcumin together create a synergistic anti-inflammatory effect stronger than either alone.

Lemon + Orange

Vitamin C from citrus stabilises curcumin and improves iron absorption. Hesperidin in orange peel adds its own anti-inflammatory flavonoid action.

Shot format

Concentrated small volume means higher bioactive density per ml. Your gut absorbs it faster on an empty stomach with less dilution.

Weekly Batch Prep Guide (7 days)
1
Scale up the batch Multiply each ingredient x7: ~7 turmeric knobs, ~7 ginger knobs, 3–4 lemons, 3–4 oranges. Keep black pepper separate — add fresh each serving.
2
Juice in this order Ginger first → Turmeric → Lemon → Orange. Citrus last to flush all the turmeric residue through the drum and protect the slow juicer.
3
Store in small dark glass bottles Fill to the brim to minimise air contact. Amber or dark green glass is ideal. Avoid plastic — turmeric oils degrade faster and stain plastic permanently.
4
Refrigerate immediately Store at 2–4°C. Potency is highest days 1–3, still very good days 4–5, and adequate through day 7. Shake well before each use as separation is natural.
5
Daily serving ritual Take one 60–80ml shot each morning on an empty stomach. Add 2–3 cracks of black pepper directly into the shot glass. Wait 15–20 min before eating for maximum absorption.
6
Clean the juicer immediately Turmeric stains within minutes. Rinse all parts with cold water right after juicing, then wash with soap. A little baking soda removes any yellow staining.

🧊 Storage Duration
How long your juice stays potent — and how to maximise it
Turmeric/Ginger Shot
5–7 days
Best in small dark glass bottles, filled to the brim. High antimicrobial content from ginger naturally preserves the blend. Shake before drinking — separation is normal.
Green Juice (kale, celery, cucumber)
24–48 hrs
Green juice oxidises fastest — chlorophyll degrades and enzymes die quickly. Drink same day for peak benefit. If storing, add a squeeze of lemon — Vitamin C slows oxidation.
Beet/Carrot/Root Juice
2–3 days
Root juices are more stable than greens. Betalains degrade after 48 hrs but are still present. Store in glass, not plastic — beet will stain and degrade faster in plastic.
Citrus Juice (lemon, orange)
2–3 days
Vitamin C actually helps it self-preserve. Store refrigerated in a sealed glass container. Avoid pre-squeezing and leaving open — Vitamin C degrades with air exposure.
Mixed Veggie Juice
1–2 days
The more ingredients, the faster degradation. Always add lemon as a natural preservative. Fill containers completely — oxygen is the enemy of fresh juice.
General Rule
Drink fresh!
Cold-press slow juicers produce less oxidation than centrifugal — your juice lasts longer. Freeze in ice cube trays if you want to extend beyond 3 days (enzymes die but nutrients survive).
💊 Supplement vs Juice — When Is Food Enough?
The honest answer to when you need a pill and when fresh juice is genuinely superior
✅ Juice Wins Here
Enzymes (Bromelain, Zingibain)Live enzymes cannot be effectively encapsulated — they degrade in capsule form and in stomach acid. Fresh juice is the only real delivery method.
Synergistic compoundsWhole food contains hundreds of co-factors that work together. Isolated curcumin supplements lack the turmeric oil matrix that helps it absorb in whole juice.
Hydration + nutrients togetherJuice delivers micronutrients dissolved in water — absorbed faster and more completely than dry capsules taken with water.
Gut microbiome supportPolyphenols and plant fibres (even in juice) feed beneficial gut bacteria — supplements can’t replicate this ecosystem effect.
Phytonutrient diversityA single juice blend delivers 50–200+ distinct phytonutrients. No supplement stack can match this breadth affordably.
💊 Supplements Win Here
Vitamin D3Almost impossible to get therapeutic amounts from food or juice. If you’re deficient (very common), supplementing 1000–2000 IU/day is non-negotiable.
Omega-3 (EPA/DHA)Plant juice gives ALA omega-3, but conversion to EPA/DHA is poor (~5%). If you don’t eat oily fish 3x/week, a fish oil or algae omega-3 supplement is justified.
MagnesiumModern soil depletion means most produce has less magnesium than 50 years ago. Magnesium glycinate supplement fills this gap that juice alone often can’t.
High-dose therapeutic curcuminFor active inflammation (arthritis, injury), therapeutic doses of 500–1000mg curcumin/day require supplements — you'd need impractical amounts of fresh turmeric juice.
B12Entirely absent from plant foods. If plant-based or low in animal products, B12 supplementation is essential — no amount of green juice provides this.
The Bottom Line
Daily fresh juice covers your anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, enzyme, and phytonutrient needs better than any supplement stack. But supplement your Vitamin D3, Omega-3, and Magnesium regardless — these are nearly impossible to get from food alone at therapeutic levels. Think of juice as your daily foundation and targeted supplements as precision top-ups.
Reference Guide
Understanding the Science
Plain-English explanations of every health term used in this guide — scroll down to read
Antioxidant
Your body constantly produces unstable molecules called free radicals from pollution, stress, and normal metabolism. Left unchecked, they damage your cells and accelerate ageing. Antioxidants neutralise free radicals — think of them as your cellular bodyguards.
Found in: beets (betalains), carrot (beta-carotene), lemon (Vit C), turmeric (curcumin)
Anti-Inflammatory
Chronic low-grade inflammation underlies almost every modern disease: heart disease, diabetes, cancer, arthritis. Anti-inflammatory compounds block specific signalling pathways (COX-2, NF-κB) that keep inflammation unnecessarily switched on. Food can genuinely act like medicine here.
Found in: turmeric (curcumin), ginger (gingerols), pineapple (bromelain), celery (apigenin)
Liver Detox
Your liver detoxifies everything in two stages — Phase I breaks toxins down; Phase II binds them for excretion. Many juice ingredients directly activate these enzyme systems. This is real, biological detox — not marketing.
Found in: beets (betaine), kale (glucosinolates), broccoli (sulforaphane), lemon (limonene)
Phytonutrients
"Phyto" means plant. These are thousands of compounds plants make to protect themselves — and when we consume them, those protective effects transfer to us. Flavonoids, carotenoids, glucosinolates, polyphenols are all phytonutrients. Different colours = different compounds — diversity matters most.
Found in: all colourful produce — stronger colour and flavour = higher phytonutrient density
Enzymes
Enzymes are biological catalysts that speed up every chemical reaction in your body. Raw juice contains live plant enzymes that survive cold-press juicing but are destroyed by heat above 40°C. They support your digestive system and reduce the energy cost of breaking down food.
Found in: pineapple (bromelain), ginger (zingibain), all raw slow-pressed produce
Bioavailability
A nutrient is only as useful as your body’s ability to actually absorb it. Curcumin alone has very low bioavailability — black pepper's piperine boosts it by ~2000%. Fat-soluble nutrients like beta-carotene absorb far better alongside a small amount of healthy fat.
Tip: turmeric + black pepper always; pair carrot juice with a small handful of nuts
